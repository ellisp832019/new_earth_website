# COLOUR_SYSTEM

## Status
Colour System v1

## Purpose
This file defines the core colour direction for the New Earth website.

The goal is to create a visual identity that feels:
- calm
- grounded
- natural
- spiritual
- intelligent
- trustworthy

The colour system should support both:
- the philosophical depth of New Earth
- the practical systems direction of the project

---

## CORE DESIGN INTENT

The New Earth palette should feel like:

- earth
- growth
- light
- balance
- warmth
- clarity

It should not feel like:
- harsh corporate tech
- neon futurism
- mystical chaos
- aggressive minimalism
- heavy darkness for the sake of drama

---

## PRIMARY PALETTE

### 1. Deep Earth Green
Use for:
- major headings
- key accents
- buttons
- important highlights
- system identity anchors

Suggested role:
This is the main “life / stewardship / growth” colour.

Example direction:
A deep forest green.

---

### 2. Warm Sand
Use for:
- background sections
- soft contrast areas
- calm visual separation
- subtle layout rhythm

Suggested role:
This is the main grounding background colour.

Example direction:
A natural sand / parchment tone.

---

### 3. Soft Off-White
Use for:
- primary page background
- readable open space
- clean text layout
- calm light sections

Suggested role:
This keeps the site breathable and uncluttered.

---

### 4. Earth Brown
Use for:
- secondary accents
- subhead emphasis
- organic balance
- grounded tonal support

Suggested role:
This adds warmth and depth without heaviness.

---

### 5. Muted Gold Accent
Use for:
- small highlights
- icon accents
- subtle emphasis
- refined section details

Suggested role:
This adds a quiet sacred / value-oriented feel without becoming flashy.

---

## PRACTICAL PALETTE ROLES

### Primary brand tone
Deep Earth Green

### Primary page background
Soft Off-White

### Alternate section background
Warm Sand

### Secondary supporting accent
Earth Brown

### Highlight / subtle premium accent
Muted Gold

---

## TEXT COLOUR RULES

### Main body text
Use a dark neutral, not pure black.

Reason:
Pure black can feel too harsh.
A softer deep charcoal reads better and feels calmer.

### Headings
Use Deep Earth Green or deep neutral depending on context.

### Muted text
Use a softer neutral brown-grey tone.

### Link colour
Use Deep Earth Green or a closely aligned darker tone.

---

## BUTTON COLOUR LOGIC

### Primary button
- background: Deep Earth Green
- text: Soft Off-White

### Secondary button
- border: Deep Earth Green
- text: Deep Earth Green
- background: transparent or very light

### Accent button use
Use Muted Gold very carefully and sparingly.

Rule:
Gold should be a detail, not the dominant interactive colour.

---

## SECTION COLOUR LOGIC

### Light doctrinal sections
Use:
- Soft Off-White background
- Deep Earth Green headings
- dark neutral body text

### Warm reflective sections
Use:
- Warm Sand background
- Earth Brown or Deep Earth Green headings

### Highlight declaration sections
Use:
- Deep Earth Green background with light text
or
- Warm Sand with strong quote styling

### Product / MicroGrow sections
Use:
- Deep Earth Green accents
- Warm Sand or Off-White backgrounds
- subtle systems styling, not hard tech styling

---

## MICROGROW COLOUR RELATIONSHIP

MicroGrow should feel like part of New Earth, not visually detached.

That means:
- use the same core palette
- slightly increase the systems feel through stronger structure
- avoid switching to generic tech blues and neon tones
- keep it aligned with nature and stewardship

MicroGrow can lean slightly more into:
- deep green
- subtle charcoal
- restrained neutral contrast

But it should remain within the New Earth colour family.

---

## EMOTIONAL DESIGN GOALS

The colours should help a visitor feel:

- settled
- open
- clear
- reflective
- grounded
- safe
- quietly inspired

The palette should support trust more than stimulation.

---

## ACCESSIBILITY RULE

All final colour choices must maintain strong readability.

Before finalizing colours, confirm:
- headings are readable
- body text contrast is strong enough
- buttons are clearly visible
- links are distinguishable
- section background changes remain subtle but clear

Beauty must not reduce clarity.
