## 3. What cookies are

Cookies are small files stored on your browser or device that help websites remember information about your visit.

Similar technologies may also include:
- pixels
- tags
- scripts
- local storage
- session technologies

For simplicity, this policy refers to all of these as cookies unless a distinction is needed.

---

## 4. How we use cookies

We may use cookies for the following purposes:

### A. Strictly necessary cookies
These are used to help the website function correctly.

They may support:
- basic page operation
- security
- login functions
- session handling
- form protection
- load balancing or technical delivery

These cookies are generally essential for the website to operate.

---

### B. Functional cookies
These may be used to remember preferences or improve the way the site works for returning visitors.

Examples may include:
- remembering interface choices
- storing simple site preferences
- improving usability

Only keep this section if such cookies are actually used.

---

### C. Analytics / performance cookies
These may be used to understand how visitors use the website so the site can be improved.

Examples may include:
- page visits
- time on page
- navigation flow
- device type
- general usage trends

Before launch, list the actual analytics tools in use.

---

### D. Embedded content / third-party cookies
If the website includes third-party content, those services may place cookies or similar tracking technologies.

Examples may include:

- maps

- third-party forms
- external media tools

Only keep the examples that actually apply.

## 6. Types of cookies we may use

Cookies may be:

### Session cookies
These are temporary and usually expire when you close your browser.

### Persistent cookies
These remain on your device for a defined period or until deleted.

- delete cookies
- clear stored site data
- control site permissions

Please note that blocking some cookies may affect how the website functions.

If a consent banner is in use, you may also be able to manage preferences there.
